Methodology

How the score is calculated

B-Box v2 uses a single, unified engine. There are no arbitrary lookup tables. Every score point traces back to a computed financial impact through a 5-year DCF cash flow model.

Step 1: Build DCF Model
A 5-year discounted cash flow model is built from your inputs: capex, revenue, opex, utilization path, inflation, and financing structure. Base IRR is computed at your stated utilization — not 100%.
Step 2: Stress Each Variable
For each of the 7 frontier variables, a stressed IRR is computed where only that variable's stress is applied. The IRR drop measures its true financial impact.
Step 3: Score from IRR Impact
Each variable is scored on an S-curve (sigmoid) based on how much IRR it erodes vs the expected maximum. The curve provides graduated diagnostic signal — small drops barely dent the score, extreme drops push it toward the floor. If the base project is already unprofitable, a continuous penalty degrades all scores further. The composite is the weighted sum.
The 7 Variables & Weights
FX Realism (25%) — capex overrun from FX gap on imported portion
Logistics & Delays (18%) — customs + FX delay reduces Year 1 operating months
Power Infrastructure (15%) — revenue loss from inadequate backup power
Inflation Trajectory (15%) — opex-revenue gap that compounds over 5 years
Working Capital (12%) — coverage adequacy vs clearance + buffer months
Maintenance Reserve (8%) — shortfall vs frontier benchmark hits opex
Technical Dependency (7%) — commissioning delays & retention issues reduce revenue
Country Context
Country modifiers adjust the stress parameters — not the scores. Ethiopia adds FX overrun and customs delay. Nigeria adds FX and inflation stress. Kenya and Rwanda reduce customs delays. Tanzania and Uganda use baseline.
Sensitivity Matrix
The 5×5 matrix recomputes the full cash flow model at each cell with revenue and opex shocks applied. No linear approximation — each cell is a fresh IRR computation.
